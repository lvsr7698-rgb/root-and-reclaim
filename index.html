I. The Dawn of Humanity

For 99% of human history, we lived as hunter-gatherers. Our behavior was cyclical, following the seasons and river migrations.

10,000 BCE: The Agricultural Revolution. Humans stopped moving and started controlling nature by diverting rivers for irrigation.

II. The River Civilizations

Civilization was born on riverbanks (Indus, Nile, Tigris). Rivers were seen as gods—givers of life and purifiers of soul.

  • Ancient View: Rivers are sacred.
  • Impact: Minimal pollution; organic waste only.
The Shift: As populations grew, human behavior shifted from "Living with Nature" to "Engineering Nature."

III. The Industrial Explosion

Starting in 1760, the relationship broke. The steam engine demanded coal. Factories demanded water—not for drinking, but for cooling and waste.

Impact: CO2 levels began their vertical climb. Forests were cleared globally for the first "Global Supply Chain."

IV. Global Warming & Impact

The 20th century saw a 1.1°C rise in global temperature. This isn't just "heat"; it's the energy unbalancing the planet.

Rivers Today: 80% of urban waste in India enters rivers untreated. Glaciers feeding the Ganga are retreating.
Weather: "Seasons" have blurred. Monsoons are shorter but more violent.

V. Human Behavior Analysis

Modern humans operate on "Instant Gratification." We have transitioned from being Keepers of Earth to Consumers of Earth.
