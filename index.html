Founder Profile

Name: SRK

Focus: Environmental History & Industrial Ethics.

This report tracks human behavior from the nomadic starting point to the current climate emergency.

Starting Point: The Nomadic Era

Human civilization began along the riverbanks. For 200,000 years, behavior was Symbiotic. We took only what we needed. Rivers like the Indus and the Nile were not just water sources; they were the foundation of our soul.

Ancient Stat: 0% Carbon Emission. 100% Biodiversity retention.

The Industrial Shift (1760+)

The Industrial Revolution changed human "Nature." We stopped seeing the Earth as a mother and started seeing it as a Warehouse. Coal mining and factory waste turned crystal rivers into industrial arteries.

  • Impact: Massive deforestation for railways.
  • Behavior: From "Worship" to "Extraction."

Climate & Global Warming

Human-induced warming has reached a critical fever pitch. The industrial impact has raised global temperatures by 1.1°C since the pre-industrial era.

Result: Melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and the death of coral reefs. Our behavior has effectively re-written the planet's biology in less than 200 years.

The Final Verdict

The monsoon patterns are failing, the rivers are drying, and the climate is shifting. SRK's research concludes that we must "Reclaim" our original respect for nature to survive the next century.
